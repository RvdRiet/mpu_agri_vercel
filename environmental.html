Environmental Enrichment Programme

Government grants and programmes for environmental conservation and sustainability in agriculture. Support soil rehabilitation, water resource management, biodiversity protection, and sustainable farming practices across Mpumalanga.

Programme Overview

What Environmental Enrichment Covers

Mpumalanga’s diverse landscapes—from the Drakensberg escarpment and Highveld grasslands to the Lowveld bushveld and river valleys—face growing pressure from soil erosion, water scarcity, and loss of indigenous vegetation. The Environmental Enrichment Programme supports farmers in adopting conservation practices that protect natural resources while improving agricultural resilience. Funding covers soil conservation measures, water efficiency infrastructure, erosion barriers, wetland protection, indigenous planting, invasive species control, and biodiversity corridors. By investing in environmental sustainability, farmers strengthen long-term productivity and contribute to the province’s conservation goals under the National Environmental Management Act (NEMA) and provincial land care initiatives.

🌱

Soil Rehabilitation

Erosion control through contour banks, gabions, and vegetative barriers. Soil testing and rehabilitation of degraded land. Support for organic matter improvement, cover cropping, and conservation tillage to combat erosion on steep escarpment slopes and communal grazing areas.

💧

Water Resource Management

Irrigation efficiency upgrades, drip systems, and rainwater harvesting structures. Wetland delineation and protection along rivers such as the Olifants, Sabie, and Crocodile. Dam rehabilitation and water storage for sustainable use in water-stressed areas.

🌲

Biodiversity & Conservation

Indigenous vegetation restoration, wildlife corridors, and buffer zones. Invasive alien plant control (e.g. wattle, gums, lantana) that threaten water yield and grazing land. Support for veld management and stewardship of natural habitats.

Grant Information

Farmer Categories and Grant Support

Grants are tailored to your farmer category. Eligible costs include fencing for conservation, erosion barriers, water infrastructure, soil testing, indigenous planting, and conservation plan development.

Farmer Category Max Grant Amount What’s Covered
Subsistence Up to R30,000 Basic conservation measures: simple erosion barriers, contour planting, soil testing, small-scale water harvesting, indigenous seed/plants for rehabilitation, basic fencing for conservation areas
Smallholder Up to R150,000 Comprehensive environmental plans: erosion control structures (gabions, contour banks), water infrastructure (irrigation efficiency, rainwater tanks), wetland buffer fencing, indigenous planting programmes, invasive species removal, conservation fencing
Commercial Up to R500,000 Subject to assessment. Larger-scale rehabilitation, wetland restoration, biodiversity corridors, comprehensive soil and water management plans, environmental impact assessments
Eligibility

Requirements & Eligibility

To qualify for environmental enrichment funding, applicants must meet the following requirements:

👤

Identity & Residence

Valid South African ID; must be a resident of Mpumalanga province or farming land within Mpumalanga. Registration as a farmer or proof of active farming/land ownership.

🏡

Land & Ownership

Proof of land access—title deed, lease agreement, PTO (Permission to Occupy), RTO (Right to Occupy), or traditional authority letter for communal land.

📋

Conservation Plan

A conservation plan or environmental management plan outlining the proposed measures, area affected, and expected outcomes. Alignment with provincial land care and NEMA requirements where applicable.

📈

Other Requirements

Not previously disqualified from departmental programmes; commitment to long-term conservation and participation in extension and monitoring; willingness to maintain conservation infrastructure.

Documents

Prepare these documents before applying. Requirements may vary by farmer category and project scale.

Proof of land Title deed, lease, PTO, RTO, or traditional authority letter Required

Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) EIA or exemption where required by NEMA for listed activities If applicable
