Crop Selection

About this grant: Approved applicants receive Smart Food Growing (SFG) systems from SFG Technologies (SFG Tec) — self-watering boxes, trays, and hydroponic units based on Square Foot Gardening principles. These systems are ideal for vegetables, herbs, and compact fruit production at home, on the farm, or in community gardens.

Definition of farmer category

"Subsistence farmer" — practices agriculture mainly for household food security. These producers are not classified as indigents by their municipality and they produce mainly for household consumption. They may market limited surplus production with an annual turnover of up to R50,000 per annum.

"Smallholder farmer" — produces for household consumption and markets, with an annual turnover ranging from R50,001 to R1,000,000.00 per annum aspiring to produce for markets and make profit.

"Commercial producer" — a commercial operation defined as an established farming venture undertaken by an individual or business entity for the purpose of production and sale of agricultural products to make a profit. The farm income must exceed R1,000,000.00 per annum.

SFG Grant Supply

Select the SFG Technologies Smart Food Growing systems you need (maximum 5 units per application). See the SFG Grant Boxes guide for product details and care instructions. Approved grants supply these units — allocation is subject to review and farmer category limits.

Supplier: SFG Technologies (SFG Tec), White River, Mpumalanga — makers of WickBox™, HydroGrow™, EcoGrow™, GrowRaft™, and VersaTray™ systems based on Square Foot Gardening principles. Units are self-watering or hydroponic, water-efficient, and suitable for indoor or outdoor use. Approved applicants also receive access to the SFG App for planting and harvesting guidance.
