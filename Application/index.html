Livestock Selection

Definition of farmer category

"Subsistence farmer" — practices agriculture mainly for household food security. These producers are not classified as indigents by their municipality and they produce mainly for household consumption. They may market limited surplus production with an annual turnover of up to R50,000 per annum.

"Smallholder farmer" — produces for household consumption and markets, with an annual turnover ranging from R50,001 to R1,000,000.00 per annum aspiring to produce for markets and make profit.

"Commercial producer" — a commercial operation defined as an established farming venture undertaken by an individual or business entity for the purpose of production and sale of agricultural products to make a profit. The farm income must exceed R1,000,000.00 per annum.

Documents & Submit

Upload required documents and review your application before submitting.

Upload one certified ID copy per listed project member. The number of required uploads is based on Total project members in Applicant Details.

Land proof confirms that you have legal access to the land where the project will be implemented.

Your business plan helps reviewers assess project feasibility, expected outputs, and sustainability.

Required for registered entities to verify financial activity and farmer category.

Provide a recent bank statement as supporting financial evidence for registered entities.

Off-take or market agreements show that you have realistic sales channels for your production.

A brand certificate is required under the Animal Identification Act for cattle, goats, sheep, and pigs to support ownership and traceability.

An EIA or environmental authorisation may be required for aquaculture operations, large piggeries, and large poultry facilities under NEMA regulations.

Required under NEMBA for farming Nile tilapia, trout, carp, and other alien species classified as Category 2 invasive species in South Africa.

Aquaculture operations drawing from natural water sources require a water use licence from DWS under the National Water Act.

A grazing plan shows how you will manage pasture rotation and carrying capacity to prevent overgrazing, as recommended by DARDLEA extension officers.

A layout plan of your poultry houses showing ventilation, feeding systems, and all-in-all-out batch management setup.

A layout plan of your piggery showing farrowing crates, grower pens, drainage, and waste management infrastructure.

Biosecurity Plan

A biosecurity plan is required by DALRRD for livestock operations. It demonstrates how you will prevent the introduction and spread of diseases on your farm, in line with the Animal Diseases Act (Act 35 of 1984) and DAFF biosecurity guidelines.

Biosecurity Plan Template

Complete each section below. This plan will be submitted with your application and assessed during farm inspection.

1. Farm Identification
2. Access Control & Visitor Management
3. Quarantine & Isolation Procedures
4. Animal Health & Veterinary Programme
5. Animal Movement & Traceability

Under the Animal Diseases Act and the Animal Identification Act (Act 6 of 2002), all livestock movements must be documented.

5. Poultry-Specific Biosecurity

Poultry biosecurity follows the DALRRD all-in-all-out principle. HPAI (Avian Influenza) is a controlled disease — any suspected case must be reported immediately.

5. Aquaculture-Specific Biosecurity

Aquaculture biosecurity focuses on water quality management, stock source control, and preventing pathogen introduction.

5. Rabbit-Specific Biosecurity
5b. Pig-Specific Biosecurity

African Swine Fever (ASF) has no vaccine — strict biosecurity is the only defence. ASF is a controlled disease under the Animal Diseases Act.

Swill feeding is illegal in South Africa under the Animal Diseases Act.

6. Feed & Water Safety
7. Carcass & Waste Disposal

Proper disposal of mortalities is a legal requirement under the Meat Safety Act and the Animal Diseases Act to prevent disease spread.

8. Controlled & Notifiable Disease Awareness

Under the Animal Diseases Act, certain diseases must be reported to the state veterinarian immediately. Failure to report is a criminal offence.
