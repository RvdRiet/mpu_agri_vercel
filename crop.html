Crop Management Programme

Comprehensive support for crop farmers across Mpumalanga. Access grants for seeds, fertilizer, equipment, and irrigation to grow maize, vegetables, fruit, sugarcane, sorghum, and more — backed by the Mpumalanga Department of Agriculture.

Programme Overview

What the Crop Management Programme Covers

Mpumalanga's diverse agro-ecological zones support a wide range of crops. Our programme provides targeted support to help farmers improve yields, adopt climate-smart practices, and access markets.

🌽

Field Crops

Maize (white and yellow), sorghum, sunflower, soya beans, dry beans, and groundnuts. Support for smallholder and commercial producers in the Highveld, Lowveld, and escarpment regions.

🍉

Vegetables

Tomatoes, cabbage, spinach, onions, carrots, potatoes, and leafy greens. Focus on market-ready production and small-scale vegetable projects for local markets and informal trade.

🍓

Fruit & Sugarcane

Citrus, avocados, macadamia, mangoes, and subtropical fruit in the Lowveld. Sugarcane and timber in Mpumalanga's eastern belt. Technical support and inputs for established and emerging growers.

Grant Information

Farmer Categories and Grant Support

Grants are tailored to your farmer category based on annual agricultural turnover. Eligible support includes seeds, fertilizer, equipment, irrigation systems, and extension services.

Farmer Category Annual Turnover Eligible Support Max Grant Amount
Subsistence Up to R50,000 Seeds, basic fertilizer, hand tools, extension advice R15,000
Smallholder R50,001 – R1,000,000 Seeds, fertilizer, equipment (planters, sprayers), irrigation support, fencing, training R75,000
Commercial Above R1,000,000 Equipment, irrigation systems, mechanisation support, market access programmes, research partnerships R250,000
Eligibility

Application Requirements

To apply for crop development support, you must meet the following requirements and provide the documents listed below.

👤

Identity & Registration

Valid South African ID; registered farmer or proof of farming activity; must be a resident of Mpumalanga province or farming within Mpumalanga.

🏡

Land Access

Proof of land access — title deed, lease agreement, PTO (Permission to Occupy), RTO (Right to Occupy), or traditional authority letter for communal land.

📋

Farming Plan

A basic farming plan outlining crops to be grown, area under production, expected yields, and how the grant will be used to improve production.

📈

Other Requirements

Not previously disqualified from departmental programmes; willingness to participate in extension and training; commitment to sustainable farming practices.

Beyond the Grant

Additional Support & Financing

Besides our crop grant, these provincial programmes can support your farming operation.

🏛

MADC Loans

The Mpumalanga Agricultural Development Corporation offers seasonal loans for inputs (seeds, fertiliser) and business loans for farm improvements. For previously disadvantaged farmers.

Tel: 013 755 6328

🌱

CASP

The Comprehensive Agricultural Support Programme provides post-settlement support including infrastructure, inputs, technical advice, and marketing links.

Ehlanzeni: 013 759 4000 · Gert Sibande: 017 647 5917 · Nkangala: 013 957 2551

💼

SEDA & LED

SEDA helps with business plans and cooperative registration. Your LED officer at the local municipality can connect you to training, funding, and workshops.

SEDA Mpumalanga: 013 755 6046 · See Support page
